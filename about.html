Who We Are

Our Story

The Beginning

From the Streets of Aragón

Zaragoza Restaurant & Bar was born from a single, powerful memory: a Sunday lunch in a sun-drenched courtyard in Aragón, surrounded by family, laughter, and dishes passed down through generations. It is that memory — that sense of warmth and belonging — that we have sought to recreate, dish by dish, since we opened our doors in 2008.

Our founders — a chef and a sommelier who met while working in a Michelin-starred restaurant in San Sebastián — believed that Spanish cuisine deserved the same reverence that French and Italian food had long enjoyed abroad. We are not a tapas bar with flamenco on the walls. We are a serious restaurant that takes its roots seriously.

Every ingredient is sourced with intention: Ibérico pigs from Extremadura, saffron from La Mancha, wines from Rioja, Ribera del Duero, and the rocky slopes of the Priorat. We partner with small producers who share our belief that the best cooking begins long before you enter the kitchen.

Our Philosophy

We Cook with Memory

"The best dish is the one that takes you somewhere — to a grandmother's kitchen, a harbor at sunset, a festive table in a village square."

We believe that a restaurant is not merely a place to eat — it is a place to gather, to slow down, and to be present. The Spanish table has always understood this. Meals are long, convivial, and layered with meaning. We have tried to build a room that invites that kind of time.

🫒
Provenance

Every ingredient tells a story. We work only with producers whose practices align with our values — seasonal, responsible, exceptional.

🍷
Tradition

Our recipes are rooted in centuries of Spanish culinary heritage. We honour the classics while finding room for interpretation and refinement.

🕯️
Hospitality

True hospitality is generosity made tangible — in the food, in the wine, in the welcome. Every guest is treated as a guest in our own home.

Meet Our Chef

Chef Alejandro Mora

Executive Chef & Co-founder

Alejandro Mora grew up in a kitchen. The son of a butcher in Zaragoza and a baker from Seville, he learned early that food is not a profession — it is a language. He spoke it fluently by the time he enrolled at the Basque Culinary Center in San Sebastián at the age of eighteen.

Over two decades, he honed his craft across some of Europe's most acclaimed kitchens: three years under Ferran Adrià's tutelage in Catalonia, a formative stage at Arzak, and five years as head chef at a Michelin-starred restaurant in Madrid. His cooking is Aragonese in its directness and Andalusian in its warmth — classical in technique, honest in spirit.

"I cook the food I miss," Alejandro says. "The food my grandmother made on Sundays. The food I ate at the market before service. The food that doesn't need a story because it is the story."

Recognition & Background
  • Basque Culinary Center graduate, San Sebastián, 2001
  • Trained under Ferran Adrià at elBulli, Catalonia, 2002–2004
  • Head Chef, Restaurante Alcázar, Madrid — 1 Michelin Star, 2005–2008
  • Co-founder, Zaragoza Restaurant & Bar, New York, 2008
  • James Beard Award nominee, Best Chef New York, 2019 & 2022
  • Zagat Top 50 New York Restaurants, 2021, 2023, 2024
